Frequently Asked Questions

Qbit is built to help people think clearly and make informed decisions. Below are answers to the most common questions.

What is Qbit?

Qbit is a reasoning-first AI that helps you understand how answers are formed. Instead of only giving results, it shows assumptions, uncertainty, and multiple perspectives so you can decide with clarity.

How is Qbit different from ChatGPT or other AI tools?

Most AI tools focus on producing fast and confident answers. Qbit focuses on transparent thinking — explaining why an answer exists, what it assumes, and where it may be uncertain.

Does Qbit give the “right” answer?

Qbit does not claim a single “right” answer. It helps surface different viewpoints and trade-offs so you can make the final decision yourself.

Who is Qbit for?

Qbit is designed for people who value clarity over speed, especially when decisions matter. It is well suited for professionals, founders, learners, and anyone dealing with complex or conflicting information.

Is Qbit free?

Qbit is currently in early access. Early users can explore core features and help shape the product. Pricing details will be shared as the product evolves.

Does Qbit store my data or conversations?

At this stage, Qbit collects only minimal information required to manage early access and improve the experience. We do not sell or share personal data. More details are available in our Privacy Policy.

Is Qbit an AI agent that takes actions on my behalf?

No. Qbit is intentionally human-in-the-loop. It helps you think and decide, but it does not execute actions for you.

Can I use Qbit for work, study, or research?

Yes. Qbit is especially useful when you need to understand complex topics, compare viewpoints, or explain reasoning clearly to others.

How can I share feedback?

Early users are encouraged to share feedback via email or feedback forms. Your input directly influences how Qbit evolves.

When will new features be added?

Features are added carefully and intentionally. Qbit prioritizes clarity and trust over rapid feature expansion.
